# Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source.

A common phishing scenario begins when a student receives an email or message that appears to be from a bank or a popular online service. The message usually creates urgency, such as claiming that the user’s account will be blocked if immediate action is not taken. It includes a link that looks legitimate but actually leads to a fake website.

When the user clicks the link, they are asked to enter their login credentials or financial details. Once entered, this information is captured by the attacker. In some cases, victims may also be asked to share OTPs, which allows attackers to complete fraudulent transactions.

Students and young internet users are often targeted because they may not always verify the authenticity of links or messages. The consequences of phishing include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

This case highlights the importance of being cautious while interacting with unknown links and messages online.
